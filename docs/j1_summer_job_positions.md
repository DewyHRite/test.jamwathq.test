## OVERVIEW

The J-1 Summer Work Travel program allows international university students to work in seasonal or temporary positions across the United States during their summer break. All positions must be entry-level, require minimal training, and provide opportunities for cultural exchange with American workers and citizens.

**Key Requirements:**
- Positions must be seasonal or temporary (not year-round)
- Entry-level, unskilled work
- Must work alongside U.S. citizens
- Maximum program duration: 4 months
- Must be pre-placed in job before entry (except visa waiver countries)

## PROHIBITED POSITIONS

**The following job types are EXPRESSLY NOT ALLOWED under J-1 regulations:**

### PROHIBITED INDUSTRIES
❌ Adult entertainment (strip clubs, escort services, adult stores)
❌ Clinical care involving patient contact
❌ Positions requiring J-1 categories (Camp Counselor, Intern, Trainee)
❌ Goods-Producing Industries (NAICS sectors 11, 21, 23, 31-33) after Nov 1, 2012

### PROHIBITED JOB TYPES
❌ Domestic help in private homes (childcare, elder care, housekeepers, gardeners, chauffeurs)
❌ Sales requiring inventory purchase
❌ Pedicab or rolling chair operators
❌ Taxi drivers or positions requiring commercial driver's license
❌ Any vehicle/vessel operation requiring driver's license
❌ Positions with traveling fairs or itinerant concessionaires
❌ Staffing agency positions (unless meeting strict criteria)
❌ Commission-only sales (must guarantee minimum wage)
❌ Jobs between 10 PM - 6 AM (late-night hours)
❌ Sustained physical contact work (massage, tattooing, body piercing, manicure)
❌ Jobs that could bring disrepute to Exchange Visitor Program
❌ Positions isolating participants from U.S. citizens
❌ Positions filling non-seasonal/non-temporary needs with staggered schedules

## POSITION REQUIREMENTS

### EMPLOYER OBLIGATIONS
Approved employers must:
- Demonstrate seasonal or temporary staffing need
- Offer entry-level positions requiring minimal training
- Provide number of hours specified in job offer
- Pay overtime per state/federal law when applicable
- Employ participants alongside U.S. citizens
- Facilitate cultural exchange opportunities
- Notify sponsor of participant arrival and any changes
- Not displace domestic U.S. workers

### PARTICIPANT REQUIREMENTS
J-1 participants must:
- Be enrolled full-time university students
- Work during official summer vacation only
- Be pre-placed before entry (except visa waiver countries)
- Work in approved seasonal/temporary position
- Interact regularly with U.S. citizens
- Complete all sponsor check-in requirements
- Maintain J-1 compliant insurance

## WAGE EXPECTATIONS BY POSITION TYPE

**Entry-Level Hospitality (Hotels/Resorts):**
- Average: $12-15/hour
- Housing: Often provided or assisted
- Tips: Additional for guest-facing roles

**Food & Beverage Service:**
- Base wage: $8-12/hour (plus tips)
- Tipped positions: $15-25/hour with tips
- Quick service: $12-15/hour

**Retail:**
- Average: $11-14/hour
- Peak season bonuses common
- Employee discounts typical

**Amusement Parks:**
- Average: $12-16/hour
- Free park admission often included
- Seasonal incentive bonuses

**Outdoor Recreation:**
- Average: $13-17/hour
- Equipment rental/use often included
- Location dependent

**Note:** Wages vary significantly by state, location, and specific employer. Cost of living varies widely across the U.S.

## HOUSING CONSIDERATIONS

**Employer-Provided Housing:**
Many resorts and hotels provide or assist with housing. When provided:
- Typically shared accommodation (2-4 per room)
- Cost: $300-600/month (deducted from paycheck)
- Often includes utilities
- Must meet local housing codes

**Self-Arranged Housing:**
If finding own housing:
- Budget: $400-800/month (varies by location)
- Consider proximity to work
- Verify transportation availability
- Confirm lease terms compatible with J-1 dates

**Housing Support:**
Sponsors must verify:
- Suitable, affordable housing availability
- Meets local codes and ordinances
- Reliable transportation to/from work
- Safe living conditions

## APPLICATION TIMELINE

**6 Months Before:**
- Research J-1 sponsor organizations
- Identify preferred U.S. locations/job types
- Begin job search (if self-placement)

**3-4 Months Before:**
- Secure job offer
- Contact J-1 sponsor for verification
- Submit application to sponsor

**2-3 Months Before:**
- Receive DS-2019 form from sponsor
- Pay SEVIS fee
- Schedule U.S. embassy visa interview

**1-2 Months Before:**
- Attend visa interview
- Receive J-1 visa in passport
- Book flights and housing
- Arrange transportation to job location

**Upon Arrival:**
- Check in with sponsor within 5 days
- Begin employment
- Attend any orientation sessions

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: Can I work more than one job?**
A: Yes, participants can work multiple jobs as long as all are verified by the sponsor and meet program requirements.

**Q: Can I change jobs after arrival?**
A: Yes, but you must notify your sponsor and have the new position verified before starting.

**Q: How many hours will I work per week?**
A: Most positions offer 30-40 hours per week. Employers must provide the hours stated in your job offer.

**Q: Will I make enough money to travel after?**
A: This varies by position, living expenses, and personal spending. Budget carefully and save throughout your program.

**Q: Can I work in my field of study?**
A: Summer Work Travel is for entry-level positions. If seeking field-specific work, consider the J-1 Internship or Trainee programs instead.

**Q: What if I have problems with my employer?**
A: Contact your J-1 sponsor immediately. They are required to assist with workplace issues.

**Q: Can my family visit while I'm working?**
A: Your family can visit on tourist visas, but you must continue working as required by your program.
