J-1 Program Overview

What is the J-1 program?

The J-1 (BridgeUSA) program is a State Department-sponsored summer work and travel initiative. It allows Jamaican university students to work abroad and experience American culture during summer break. Participants typically work in hospitality or tourism and engage in cultural exchange.


Who Can Apply?

Eligible applicants must be full-time students at a Jamaican university pursuing a 4-year Bachelor of Science degree with at least a 2.0 GPA. Students must secure acceptance with an approved program sponsor, demonstrate adequate English skills, obtain valid health insurance, and complete all required program documentation.

They offer structured internships, training, and work opportunities that help participants gain practical skills and experience American workplace culture. These employers must meet program standards and work with designated sponsors to ensure the experience is educational and culturally meaningful, not just a regular job.

Summary

J-1 (BrigdeUSA) Program

The J-1 Exchange Visitor Program is a U.S. program for nonimmigrant visas that promotes international education and culture. It welcomes international participants to the US for professional training, academic study, teaching, research, or cultural activities. The State Department authorizes a network of public and private entities as program sponsors. The J-1 visa has many categories for diverse participant characteristics and goals. Intern/trainee, student/secondary school, teacher, professor, researcher, specialist, and cultural programs like Au Pair, Camp Counselor, and Summer Work Travel are common. Each category is meant to help participants enter the U.S. in a way that fosters personal growth and cultural interchange. The sponsor's DS-2019 form (Certificate of Eligibility) is essential, confirming the participant's program, stay, and J-1 visa eligibility. Employers and schools provide a practical environment for participants to work, study, and experience U.S. workplace culture. Cultural interchange is a key component of the J-1 program, encouraging participants to learn about American culture and share their own. SEVIS tracks J-1 participants' status, compliance, and movements during their stay to ensure the program meets its goals and host organizations meet regulatory requirements.
